Skateistan began as a grassroots 'Sport for Development' project on the streets of Kabul in 2007.
Skateistan is the first international development initiative to combine skateboarding with educational outcomes.
Skateistan use skateboarding as a tool for empowering youth, to create new opportunities and the potential for change.
Oliver Percovich dropped his board in Kabul in 2007, he was surrounded by the eager faces of children of all ages who wanted to be shown how to skate.
A group of Afghan friends (aged 18-22) who were naturals at skateboarding shared the three boards and quickly progressed in their new favourite sport.
Skateistan believes that when youth come together to skateboard and play, they forge bonds that transcend social barriers.
